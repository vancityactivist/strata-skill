# British Columbia — Strata Property Act (SBC 1998, c. 43)

Reference for strata management duties under BC's Strata Property Act (SPA), Strata Property Regulation, and the Real Estate Services Act (RESA, which licenses strata managers).

> Verify section numbers against the current consolidated act at bclaws.gov.bc.ca before quoting in formal correspondence — amendments are frequent (e.g., rental and age-restriction bylaw changes in 2022).

## Key actors

- **Strata corporation** — the legal entity comprising all owners (s. 2).
- **Strata council** — elected body exercising the corporation's powers between general meetings (ss. 4, 26).
- **Strata manager / brokerage** — licensed under RESA; acts under a written strata management contract (SPA ss. 24–25 govern delegation).

## Meetings

| Item | Requirement |
|---|---|
| AGM timing | Within 2 months of fiscal year end (s. 40) |
| Notice of AGM/SGM | At least 14 days' written notice + 4 days deemed mailing time (ss. 45, 61) |
| Quorum | Persons holding 1/3 of votes (s. 48) |
| Demand for SGM | 20% of votes may demand one; must be held within 4 weeks (s. 43) |
| Majority vote | Most decisions |
| 3/4 vote | Bylaw amendments (s. 128), special levies (s. 108), significant changes to common property (s. 71), CRF expenditures outside the depreciation-report exception (s. 96) |
| Unanimous vote | Limited matters, e.g., changes to unit entitlement in some cases |

## Money

- **Budget**: approved at each AGM by majority vote (s. 103).
- **Contingency reserve fund (CRF)**: mandatory; minimum contributions per Regulation; spending generally requires 3/4 vote unless related to a depreciation-report recommendation (majority) or emergency (s. 96, s. 98).
- **Unapproved expenditures (s. 98)**: only within bylaw/regulation limits or in emergencies to ensure safety / prevent significant loss.
- **Special levies (s. 108)**: 3/4 vote; levied by unit entitlement unless a different formula is unanimously approved.
- **Depreciation reports (s. 94)**: required on a 5-year cycle (as amended); check current Regulation for exemptions and timing.

## Records and disclosure

- **Records to keep** (s. 35) and retention periods (Regulation 4.1); owners' right to inspect/copy (s. 36).
- **Form B Information Certificate (s. 59)**: deliver within 7 days of request.
- **Form F Payment Certificate (s. 115)**: required for conveyance; 7 days.

## Bylaws and enforcement

- Standard Bylaws apply unless amended (s. 120); amendments need 3/4 vote and must be filed at the Land Title Office before enforceable (s. 128).
- Enforcement procedure (s. 135): give particulars in writing and a reasonable opportunity to respond (and a hearing if requested) **before** imposing a fine — fines imposed without this are invalid.
- Fine limits set by Regulation (commonly $200/contravention; $50–$500 ranges depending on bylaw type; check current values).

## Repairs and insurance

- Corporation repairs/maintains common property and common assets (s. 72); owners repair their strata lots (Standard Bylaw 2 vs. 8 split).
- Corporation must insure common property and buildings to full replacement value (ss. 149–155); review deductible bylaws and s. 158 deductible-chargeback to owners.

## Disputes

- **Civil Resolution Tribunal (CRT)** has jurisdiction over most strata disputes — bylaws, governance, repairs, finances.
- Court remains for some matters (e.g., s. 173 orders, significant unfairness claims can go either path — verify current jurisdictional split).

## Strata manager licensing duties (RESA)

- Written service agreement required; trust accounting rules for strata funds; duty to act in the client (strata corporation)'s best interest; disclosure of conflicts (e.g., commissions on insurance placement must be disclosed).
