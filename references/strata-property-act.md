# British Columbia — Strata Property Act (SBC 1998, c. 43)

Reference for strata management duties under BC's Strata Property Act (SPA), Strata Property Regulation, and the Real Estate Services Act (RESA, which licenses strata managers).

> Verify section numbers against the current consolidated act at bclaws.gov.bc.ca before quoting in formal correspondence — amendments are frequent (e.g., rental and age-restriction bylaw changes in 2022).

## Key actors

- **Strata corporation** — the legal entity comprising all owners (s. 2).
- **Strata council** — elected body exercising the corporation's powers between general meetings (ss. 4, 26).
- **Strata manager / brokerage** — licensed under RESA; acts under a written strata management contract. Delegation limits are set by the bylaws (Standard Bylaw 20); strata management contracts made before the first AGM are cancellable under s. 24.

## Meetings

| Item | Requirement |
|---|---|
| AGM timing | Within 2 months of fiscal year end (s. 40) |
| Notice of AGM/SGM | At least 14 days' written notice + 4 days deemed mailing time (ss. 45, 61) |
| Quorum | Eligible voters holding 1/3 of votes, in person or by proxy; 2/3 if fewer than 4 strata lots (s. 48) |
| Demand for SGM | 20% of votes may demand one; must be held within 4 weeks (s. 43) |
| Majority vote | Most decisions |
| 3/4 vote | Bylaw amendments (s. 128), special levies (s. 108), significant changes to common property (s. 71), CRF expenditures outside the s. 96 majority-vote exceptions |
| Unanimous vote | Limited matters, e.g., changes to unit entitlement in some cases |

## Money

- **Budget**: approved at each AGM by majority vote (s. 103).
- **Contingency reserve fund (CRF)**: mandatory; the annual contribution must be at least 10% of the operating fund budget (Reg. 6.1, effective Nov 1, 2023 — up from the old 5%/25%-cap formula). Spending requires a 3/4 vote, except a **majority vote** suffices for: depreciation reports, repairs/replacements recommended in the current depreciation report, EV charging infrastructure, and electrical planning reports (s. 96).
- **Unapproved expenditures (s. 98)**: from the operating fund, up to the bylaw limit — or if the bylaws are silent, the lesser of $2,000 or 5% of the current year's operating fund contribution. From either fund in a genuine emergency (immediate expenditure necessary to ensure safety or prevent significant loss or damage), limited to the minimum amount needed.
- **Special levies (s. 108)**: 3/4 vote if levied by the s. 99/100 formula (unit entitlement); a different fair division requires a unanimous vote. The resolution must state purpose, total amount, method, each lot's share, and payment dates.
- **Depreciation reports (ss. 92, 94; Reg. 6.2–6.22)**: required at least once every 5 years (Reg. 6.21(2)); only corporations with fewer than 5 lots are exempt (Reg. 6.22). Reports obtained on or after July 1, 2025 must be prepared by a prescribed qualified professional (engineer, architect, applied science technologist, or accredited appraiser). Transitional deadlines: July 1, 2026 (designated areas) / July 1, 2027 (elsewhere); new corporations get 2 years from first AGM (18 months if established after July 1, 2027).
- **Liens (s. 116)**: only strata fees, special levies, s. 85 reimbursements, and a lot's share of a judgment are lienable. **Fines and remediation costs are not lienable** (s. 116(3)) — they must be recovered through the CRT or court.

## Records and disclosure

- **Records to keep** (s. 35) and retention periods (Regulation 4.1); owners' right to inspect/copy (s. 36).
- **Form B Information Certificate (s. 59)**: deliver within 1 week of request. Since April 2023 it must include a summary of the strata corporation's insurance coverage, alongside the rules, current budget, and most recent depreciation report.
- **Form F Certificate of Payment (s. 115)**: required for conveyance; deliver within 1 week.

## Bylaws and enforcement

- Standard Bylaws apply unless amended (s. 120); amendments need 3/4 vote and must be filed at the Land Title Office before enforceable (s. 128).
- Enforcement procedure (s. 135): give particulars in writing and a reasonable opportunity to respond (and a hearing if requested) **before** imposing a fine — fines imposed without this are invalid.
- Fine limits (Reg. 7.1): maximum $200 per bylaw contravention, $50 per rule contravention, and $1,000 per contravention of a short-term-rental bylaw. Continuing contraventions: repeat fines no more often than every 7 days — but daily for short-term-rental bylaws (Reg. 7.1(2)).
- Rental restrictions: since the November 2022 amendments (Bill 44), strata corporations cannot restrict rentals or screen tenants (s. 141 as replaced); existing rental-restriction bylaws are unenforceable. Bylaws restricting short-term rentals (under 30 days) remain valid. Age-restriction bylaws survive only as 55+ (s. 123.1), with caregiver and grandfathering exemptions (s. 123.2).

## Repairs and insurance

- Corporation repairs/maintains common property and common assets (s. 72); owners repair their strata lots (Standard Bylaw 2 vs. 8 split).
- Corporation must insure common property, common assets, buildings, and original fixtures to full replacement value (s. 149); liability insurance also mandatory (s. 150).
- Insurance review and reporting (s. 154, strengthened by the 2020 Bill 14 amendments): review adequacy annually, report on coverage at each AGM, and inform owners and tenants as soon as feasible of material changes in coverage, including deductible increases.
- Deductibles (s. 158): a common expense, but the corporation may sue an owner to recover the deductible if the owner is responsible for the loss (s. 158(2)); many corporations also have deductible-chargeback bylaws.

## Disputes

- **Civil Resolution Tribunal (CRT)** has jurisdiction over most strata disputes — bylaws, governance, repairs, finances.
- Court remains for some matters (e.g., s. 173 orders, significant unfairness claims can go either path — verify current jurisdictional split).

## Strata manager licensing duties (RESA)

- Written service agreement required; trust accounting rules for strata funds; duty to act in the client (strata corporation)'s best interest; disclosure of conflicts (e.g., commissions on insurance placement must be disclosed).
