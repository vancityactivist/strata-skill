# Template: Notice of Bylaw Complaint (s. 135 Notice to Comply)

## Usage notes (not part of the letter)

- This is the **first formal step** of the s. 135 procedure. No fine may be imposed until the recipient has had the particulars in writing and a reasonable opportunity to answer, including a hearing if requested. Skipping or rushing this invalidates the fine.
- Address it to the owner; if a tenant is involved, send to both (s. 135(1)).
- Be specific in the particulars: date, time, location, what was observed, and which bylaw or rule it contravenes. Vague particulars are the most common reason fines fail at the CRT.
- Set a response deadline that is genuinely reasonable — 14 days is the customary floor.
- Do not state that a fine *has been* imposed; the decision comes after the response window closes.
