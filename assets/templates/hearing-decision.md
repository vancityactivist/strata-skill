# Template: Council Decision Following Hearing or Response (s. 135 / s. 34.1)

## Usage notes (not part of the letter)

- Two distinct uses — adapt the opening accordingly:
  - **s. 135(2)**: the decision on a bylaw complaint after the owner/tenant responded or was heard. Must be given in writing "as soon as feasible."
  - **s. 34.1**: the decision after a requested council hearing on any matter — if a decision was requested, it is due in writing **within 1 week** of the hearing.
- State the council's reasons briefly and factually. Reasons demonstrate the council actually considered the answer given — the thing the CRT looks for.
- If a fine is imposed: state the amount, the bylaw, the due date, and (if applicable) that continuing contraventions may attract further fines every 7 days without further notice (s. 135(3)). Remember fines are not lienable — do not threaten a lien.
- If no fine is imposed or the complaint is dismissed, say so plainly — closing the loop in writing protects everyone.
- Include the recipient's recourse (CRT) — fairness here reads well later.
